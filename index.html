About

Hey there! I'm Satchit, a CS student from Chennai, India. I've always wanted to be so many different things - a writer, a web-comic artist, an animator, a photographer. Then in high school, I found out I could code. What's more, I had incredible fun doing it.

It was a natural progression from then on, because games were right up there with reading as one of my favorite things to do. I wanted to make games that would make people feel the way my favorite games made me feel.

I got into the CS undergraduate program in SSN College of Engineering. In my second year, I started making games with Unity3D for mobile. It was really satisfying to see people play my games and having fun with them, even if they were 'only mobile games.'

I interned in an AR/VR startup called Dreamware for a few months in 2015-16, and in another startup, Kaleidozone in Summer 2016. My idea of a dream job still remains the same - make great games that touch people's lives, and move them in the way that only great art can.

Meta

Coding
Level Design
Art

PLAY STORE LINK

Meta is an implementation of ultimate tic-tac-toe, a much more complex (and more importantly, unsolved) variation of tic-tac-toe. There are nine 3x3 tic-tac-toe boards, and each move you make on the little board decides which board the oppponent plays on (if you played on the top-right corner of the top-left board in your first move, the opponent can play in any non-filled square of the top-right board on his turn). Each mini-board is won according to normal rules. To win the game, players have to win three mini-boards in a line. The algorithm used was a variation of minimax search with alpha-beta pruning (optimzed for the particular problem), implemented using stacks.

Spartan is a minimalist 2D side-scrolling endless runner reminiscent of Jetpack Joyride(or if you spent a large chunk of your childhood playing Flash games like me, Copter). Spartan features a main character who moves up if the screen is touched, and falls down under gravity otherwise. There are randomly generated lasers to avoid, and randomly generated coins to collect. The character has a cute fire under him, and his arms flail about when moving up and down, the coins rotate, and there are randomly generated bulbs and boxes for aesthetic appeal... and that's about it. It's about as barebones as a mobile game can get. But that doesn't stop it from being fun!

Blob Rush

Coding
Level Design
Art

PLAY STORE LINK

Blob Rush is a game based on Sebastian Lague's awesome Create a game series. It's a top-down 3D wave-based survival shooter, adapted for mobile controls and convinience. Apart from the features Seb implements, which include randomly generated levels, player-seeking enemies, scaling difficulty, enemy hit-animation(using code), gun and bullet physics, muzzle flash etc, I added an aim assist and a touch control scheme which includes a virtual joystick for moving and another pad which takes the shooting direction as input(tapping the top left of the pad shoots the enemy closest to that direction within a margin of error). I also modified the enemy gore by using a randomized cube generator and adding random forces to it because the particle system caused issues on mobile, and added toon shaders for effect.

Car Simulator

Coding
Level Design

A college-funded project, this car simulator uses a Leap Motion controller, Google Cardboard, Makey Makey and a software called Trinus VR to provide an immersive car driving experience for learners. The application was made in Unity using free, open-source models, which I animated with scripting. The Leap-Motion is used for steering and shifting gears. The Makey Makey chip interfaces three makeshift pedals to the PC, mapping them to keys on the keyboard. Trinus VR streams the PC display onto an Android device, which can then be used with Cardboard for the full VR experience.

Liver VR Demo

Coding
Level Design
UI

A demo application made for Oculus that illustrates the benefits of the liver. The camera splines through a complex 3D environment, before coming to rest at a model of the human body's interior. A Leap Motion controller is used to get input from the user. Flipping the user's left hand leads to a menu showing up near the hand, on which there are three sub-menus. The menu items can be selected by the right hand's fingers. The sub-menu options display text on a world space HUD, change the model of the liver and play some voiceover clips. Moving a balled fist horizontally rotates the model about its y axis, and moving pinched fingers in and out(z-axis) scales the model down and up respectively.

AchARya AR Demo

Coding
Level Design
UI

Developed in 32 hours for the SSN 32-hour startup challenge, AchARya(teacher in Sanskrit) is a prototype for an AR-based educational app that complements traditional learning media, such as text books. For topics that are too difficult to understand just via text and 2D image, the AR app brings up a 3D model, an animation, or a relevant video tutorial when the camera is pointed at that particular page in the book. This will engage students better and help them learn immersively. For the demo, two 3D models and one video were incorporated as an example. The app won best UI prize at the contest.
