Splatoon2D


Link to github

Team size : 2

Splatoon2D is a Splatoon-inspired 4-player free-for-all where the objective is to fill up the largest portion of the screen with your color. Each player has HP and paint ammo, and can paint within a radius around them. When standing on their own paint, players can go into 'squid mode', where they regenerate paint much faster, and move quicker too. When standing on enemy paint, players move slower and take damage-over-time. If an enemy fires on top of the player, they take extra damage, and once they die, they are out of the game for five seconds (then they respawn).

Main contributions:

  • Grid-based spray-painting system with realistic brush strokes and spray-paint effect

  • Generic sprite renderer

  • Movement, attacking, player damage

  • Animation programming

  • HP, paint and score UI

Color Switch


Link to github

Color Switch is a clone of the popular mobile game written in Motorola 68000 assembly code. It was an individual class assignment. The objective is to make the ball go as far as possible. Hitting the floor or an obstacle of a different color as the ball ends the session. There’s only one input – pressing space makes the ball jump. The ball is under constant gravity. The color of the ball changes after every obstacle is crossed. Salient features include:

  • Rotation for objects in assembly (using sine and cosine look-up tables generated manually)

  • Notion of reusable classes and objects that can be spawned from them in assembly

  • Time-based gameplay and collisions (rather than frame-based)

  • Emulation of fixed-point math in registers

Terrain Generation with improved Perlin noise


Link to github

This is a personal project that includes a texture generator and a terrain generator, which employ the improved Perlin noise algorithm to generate a 3D mesh that mimics natural terrain.

Features:

  • Improved 3D Perlin noise algorithm based on Ken Perlin's original paper and implementation

  • Fully configurable seed value, frequency, octaves, lacunarity, and persistence via the Unity inspector (editor script)

  • Configurable color gradient for changing colors based on height

  • Animated water that moves through the layers of 3D perlin noise and deforms the mesh

Geometric 2D Lights and Shadows


Click to play the demo! (use wasd to move)


Link to github

This is a personal project where I attempt to recreate dynamic 2D shadows present in stealth games such as Monaco and Mark of the Ninja.

Features:

  • Shadows/light 2D meshes generated based only on Unity colliders

  • Optimized algorithm keeps a list of existing colliders in the light/shadow bounds at all times and cycles through them clock-wise, avoiding expensive ray-casts.

  • Source of shadows/light can move about, rotate, overlap etc, so can objects that obstruct vision

  • Linear gradient shader for softer edges to shadows

Meta

Coding
Level Design
Art

PLAY STORE LINK

Meta is an implementation of ultimate tic-tac-toe, a much more complex (and more importantly, unsolved) variation of tic-tac-toe. There are nine 3x3 tic-tac-toe boards, and each move you make on the little board decides which board the oppponent plays on (if you played on the top-right corner of the top-left board in your first move, the opponent can play in any non-filled square of the top-right board on his turn). Each mini-board is won according to normal rules. To win the game, players have to win three mini-boards in a line. The algorithm used was a variation of minimax search with alpha-beta pruning (optimzed for the particular problem), implemented using stacks.

  • Installs: 143
  • Rating: 4.96/5

Liver VR Demo

Coding
Level Design

A demo application made for Oculus that illustrates the benefits of the liver. The camera splines through a complex 3D environment, before coming to rest at a model of the human body's interior. A Leap Motion controller is used to get input from the user. Flipping the user's left hand leads to a menu showing up near the hand, on which there are three sub-menus. The menu items can be selected by the right hand's fingers. The sub-menu options display text on a world space HUD, change the model of the liver and play some voiceover clips. Moving a balled fist horizontally rotates the model about its y axis, and moving pinched fingers in and out(z-axis) scales the model down and up respectively.
